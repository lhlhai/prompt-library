📚 Prompt Library

📖 User Guide

Learn how to get the most out of Prompt Library

Getting Started

1. Browse Prompts

Start by exploring the library. You can view all prompts in grid or list view. Use the view toggle buttons in the top right to switch between different layouts.

2. Search & Filter

Use the search bar to find prompts by name, description, or content. You can also filter by categories using the sidebar on the left. Combine multiple filters for more precise results.

3. View Full Prompt

Click the "👁️ View Full" button on any prompt card to open a detailed view. Here you can see the complete prompt, description, usage guidelines, and more.

4. Copy Prompts

Click the "📋 Copy" button to copy a prompt to your clipboard. You can choose to copy as plain text or with Markdown formatting. The prompt is ready to paste into your AI tool of choice.

5. Save Favorites

Click the star icon (⭐ or ☆) to add a prompt to your favorites. Your favorite prompts will be saved in your browser's local storage and appear in the "Quick Access" section.

6. Edit Prompts

In the detailed view, click "✍️ Edit" to customize a prompt. Your changes are saved locally and don't affect the original prompt. Click "🔄 Reset" to restore the original version.

7. Compare Prompts

Select multiple prompts using the compare checkbox and click "📊 Compare Now" to view them side-by-side. This is useful for choosing between similar prompts or understanding different approaches.

8. Share Prompts

Click the "🔗 Share" button to generate a shareable link. You can send this link to colleagues, and they'll see the specific prompt with your edits (if any).

Tips & Tricks
  • Use Recently Used: Access your most recently used prompts from the Quick Access section
  • Keyboard Shortcuts: Use Ctrl+K (or Cmd+K on Mac) to focus the search bar
  • View Preferences: Your view preference (grid/list) is saved automatically
  • Local Storage: All your favorites and edits are stored locally in your browser
  • Download Prompts: Click "⬇️ Download .txt" to save a prompt as a text file
FAQ

Where are my favorites saved?

Your favorites are saved in your browser's local storage. They persist across sessions but are specific to your browser and device.

Can I sync my data across devices?

Currently, data is stored locally. For cross-device sync, you can export your favorites and import them on another device (feature coming soon).

How do I suggest a new prompt?

Visit our GitHub repository and open an issue or submit a pull request with your prompt.

Can I use these prompts commercially?

Yes! The prompts are available under the MIT License, so you can use them commercially. Just include the license notice in your project.
